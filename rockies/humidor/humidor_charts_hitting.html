The so-called juiced baseballs in the 2019 season resulted in 6,776 home runs hit during the regular season, which shattered the previous record, set in 2017, by nearly 11%. Baseball's "steroid era" is generally considered to have run from the late 1980s through the late 2000s and certainly impacted both hitting and pitching at Coors Field. Although steroids have been banned in Major League Baseball since 1991, MLB did not implement league-wide PED testing until 2003, a year after the Rockies began using the humidor at Coors Field.
